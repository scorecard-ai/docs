At the core of Scorecard is the principle of metrics and scoring. At Scorecard we've found the process of defining what 'good' performance looks like and selecting a set of metrics to codify that behavior is crucial in understanding and improving the performance of your LLM app. Metrics serve as the benchmarks for assessing the quality of LLM responses, while scoring is the process of applying these metrics to generate actionable insights about your LLM app.

## Selecting metrics

There are diverse types of metrics available in the Scorecard ecosystem:

1. **Scorecard Core Metrics:** These are best-in-class, industry-standard metrics validated through testing by Scorecard. 

2. **Second-party Metrics:** These encompass metrics from ecosystem partners such as RAGAS and MLflow.

3. **Custom Metrics:** Scorecard also offers the flexibility to define any customized metrics unique to specific needs.

From this set, we can validate custom metrics, and eventually expand into a Large-Scale Testset. Using a Large-Scale Testset and Metrics, Scorecard can automatically evaluate the quality of your LLM application's responses and provide you with actionable feedback.

## Scoring config

Once a set of metrics that best evaluates your LLM application is finalized, these metrics can be grouped together into a Scoring Configuration. This configuration can be run on a regular basis against your LLM application to yield a consistent measure of the performance and quality of its responses.
